Attack Surface

About This Model

Your attack surface is the sum of all points where an attacker can try to enter or extract data. Every feature, API endpoint, third-party integration, dependency, and line of code expands it. More surface = more vulnerability.

Why it matters: The average web application has 26 third-party integrations (Snyk 2023). Each one is a potential breach vector. 80% of breaches involve third-party or supply chain vulnerabilities.

Your attack surface grows with every feature, integration, and dependency. Hover over each bubble to see how complexity compounds.

Key Insights

  • Complexity is the enemy: Every new feature, integration, or dependency increases attack surface non-linearly. 4 components â†’ 6 connections. 10 components â†’ 45 connections.
  • Third-party risk is your risk: You don't control their security, but you're liable for breaches. SolarWinds, Log4j, and MOVEit were all supply chain attacks.
  • Unused code is still vulnerable: That deprecated API endpoint you forgot about? Still exposed. That old library you never removed? Still exploitable.
  • Reduction is a feature: Every feature you don't ship, every integration you don't add, every dependency you remove makes you more secure.
  • Real-world example: SolarWinds breach (2020) compromised 18,000 organizations through a single supply chain attack. One compromised dependency = massive attack surface expansion.
